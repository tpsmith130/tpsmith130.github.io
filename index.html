Signs of an overdose

What is Nalaxone?

Nalaxone is medication designed to rapidly reverse opioid overdose. Considered an opioid antagonist which means that it blinds the receptors in the brain that will receive the opioid and can reverse/block the effects of opioids. Quickly restores respiration to those who have slowed or stopped breathing due to overdose.

If you are worried for yourself or a loved one here are some signs and symptoms of opioid Overdose:

  • Confusion, delirium, or acting drunk
  • Mood Swings
  • Nausea or vomiting
  • Extreme constipation
  • Pinpoint pupils
  • Extreme sleepiness, or the inability to wake up
  • Breathing problems, including slowed or irregular breathing
  • Stopped breathing or depressed breathing (one of the most dangerous due to lack of oxygen to the brain)

How is Nalaxone administered?

Nalaxone can be administered as an auto-injectable (EVZIO) and is also available as a prepackaged nasal spray (NARCAN). The injectable form of Naloxone (EVZIO) is commonly administered by paramedics and those who are trained to use it. The nasal spray form of Naloxone (NARCAN) is available over the counter and can be administered by anyone. If you or someone you know is worried of someone experiencing a possible opioid overdose, it is recommended to obtain NARCAN from wherever it is available. If someone you know is overdosing on opioids and NARCAN is not around, call paramedics as soon as possible for they can help reverse the effects of the overdose by administering EVZIO.

What needs to happen after administering Naloxone?

Those who have been given naloxone in any form should be transferred to a hospital where they can help withdraw the drug from the individual’s system. Although Naloxone can help reverse and block the effects of an opioid overdose, the effects of Naloxone do not last forever and can wear off within 30-90 minutes of being administered. Naloxone can cause withdrawal symptoms which can be uncomfortable, but are not life-threatening. This is an extremely safe, non-habit-forming medication that can help save someone’s life.
